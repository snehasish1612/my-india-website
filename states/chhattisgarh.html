Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, often called the “Rice Bowl of Central India,” is a state of lush forests, majestic waterfalls, and vibrant tribal traditions. Known for its handlooms, handicrafts, and ancient temples, it offers a blend of nature and heritage. The state’s rich biodiversity makes it a hidden treasure for eco-tourism.

Capital: Raipur
Pop: ~32M

Fun Facts
  • - Home to “Niagara Falls of India” — Chitrakote Falls.
  • - 44% of its land is covered by forests.
  • - Famous for Kosa silk and bell metal crafts.
  • - Bastar Dussehra is the longest festival in the world (75 days).
